Do These Sound Familiar?

Anxiety shows up differently for everyone. You might experience some or all of these symptoms:

Constant worry that won't stop
Racing thoughts, especially at night
Feeling on edge or easily startled
Physical tension, headaches, or stomach issues
Avoiding situations that make you anxious
Difficulty concentrating or sleeping
Panic attacks or intense fear
Overthinking and second-guessing decisions

If you recognize yourself in any of these, anxiety therapy can help you break the cycle.

Our Approach to Anxiety Treatment

We use proven, evidence-based approaches tailored to your specific type of anxiety and personal needs.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Identify and challenge the thought patterns that fuel your anxiety. Learn to replace catastrophic thinking with realistic, balanced perspectives.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Build distress tolerance and emotional regulation skills. Learn mindfulness techniques to stay grounded when anxiety spikes.

Relaxation & Mindfulness

Practical techniques for calming your nervous system — breathing exercises, progressive muscle relaxation, and grounding practices.

Exposure Therapy

Gradually and safely face the situations you've been avoiding, building confidence and reducing fear one step at a time.

Types of Anxiety We Treat

Generalized Anxiety

Persistent, excessive worry about everyday things — work, health, family, finances. Learn to quiet the "what ifs" and find peace.

Social Anxiety

Intense fear of social situations or being judged. Build confidence and social skills to engage comfortably with others.

Panic Disorder

Sudden, intense panic attacks that feel overwhelming. Learn to manage and prevent panic, and stop fearing the next one.

Health Anxiety

Excessive worry about illness or physical symptoms. Break the cycle of checking, googling, and catastrophizing.

OCD-Related Anxiety

Intrusive thoughts and compulsive behaviors that disrupt daily life. Evidence-based treatment can help you regain control.

Performance Anxiety

Fear of tests, presentations, or performing. Build confidence and coping strategies to show up as your best self.
