Is Your Child Struggling?

Children often can't express what they're feeling in words. Watch for these signs that your child might benefit from therapy:

Mood Changes

Increased sadness, irritability, anger outbursts, or emotional withdrawal that seems out of character.

Sleep or Appetite Changes

Trouble falling asleep, nightmares, eating significantly more or less than usual.

School Difficulties

Declining grades, refusing to go to school, trouble with teachers or classmates.

Social Withdrawal

Avoiding friends, loss of interest in activities they used to enjoy, isolating in their room.

Behavioral Issues

Defiance, aggression, tantrums beyond what's age-appropriate, or sudden rule-breaking.

Anxiety Signs

Excessive worry, physical complaints (stomachaches, headaches), avoidance of new situations.

Our Therapeutic Approaches

Family Involvement

Parents are partners in the process. We'll help you support your child's progress at home.

A Note for Parents

Seeking therapy for your child isn't a sign of failure — it's a sign of love. You're giving them tools and support that will serve them for life. We'll work together as a team, keeping you informed and involved while respecting your child's need for a safe, confidential space to open up.
